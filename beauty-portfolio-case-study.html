Project Overview

Lumi√®re Beauty addresses a critical pain point in the beauty industry: the overwhelming challenge of finding makeup products that match individual skin tones. With limited in-store testing during the pandemic and countless online options, customers waste money on mismatched products. This project explores how intelligent color matching technology can create a personalized, confident shopping experience.

The Challenge

How might we help beauty consumers discover products perfectly matched to their unique skin tone, undertone, and personal style without the trial-and-error of traditional shopping?

The Solution

A luxury beauty subscription service with an intelligent quiz that adapts its visual interface to match the user's skin tone, creating a personalized preview of how products will complement their complexion.

User Research Insights

Interviews with 20 beauty consumers revealed three critical insights:

  • Color Matching Anxiety 73% of participants had purchased foundation in the wrong shade online. The inability to see how products look against their skin creates purchasing hesitation and high return rates.
  • Overwhelm from Options With 40+ foundation shades per brand, users feel paralyzed by choice. They want expert guidance but personalized to their unique needs.
  • Lack of Undertone Understanding Most users couldn't identify whether they had warm, cool, or neutral undertones, leading to mismatched purchases despite correct shade depth.
  • Trust in Visual Preview Users expressed significantly more confidence when they could see products displayed against backgrounds similar to their skin tone.

Key Features & Innovations

1. Intelligent Color Matching Quiz

The three-question quiz efficiently gathers essential information while providing an engaging, personalized experience.

  • Question 1: Skin Tone (8 options) Visual swatches allow users to select their foundation shade. White background ensures accurate color perception without interference.
  • Question 2: Undertone (Warm/Cool/Neutral) Illustrated icons replace emojis for professional appearance. Background adapts to selected skin tone, previewing product compatibility.
  • Question 3: Beauty Goals (4 styles) Natural Glow, Bold & Glamorous, Professional, or Experimental preferences guide product curation.

  • Progressive Background Application White background on Question 1 for color accuracy, then seamless transition to matched background on Questions 2-3 and results.

  • Multi-Section Navigation SPA-style navigation between Home, About, Quiz, and Results without page reloads for smooth user experience.

Key Learnings & Reflections

  • Color Science Matters Understanding undertones and how they interact with product shades was crucial. The adaptive background system works because it's based on actual color theory, not arbitrary choices.
  • White Background for Selection is Critical Initially considered using branded backgrounds throughout, but realized users need a neutral backdrop to accurately perceive and select their skin tone. This insight came from color psychology research.
  • Inclusive Design Requires Research The eight skin tone ranges were carefully calibrated by studying existing inclusive beauty brands (Fenty, Huda Beauty) and ensuring adequate representation across the spectrum.
  • Match Percentages Build Confidence Displaying 92-98% match scores transformed the experience from "hoping this works" to "confident in my choices." The specific percentages (not just "high match") created credibility.
  • Flexibility Over Commitment Adding individual product purchasing alongside subscription options reduced friction. Users appreciated the flexibility to try products before committing to monthly boxes.
  • Professional Icons Over Emojis Initial emoji icons felt too casual for a luxury beauty brand. Custom CSS illustrations elevated the design and maintained brand sophistication.
